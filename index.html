About me

I'm a researcher at the University of Catania working on real-time processing of positioning data from fixed cameras and mobile GNSS sensors. My work focuses on Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) and its applications in advancing data science.

With a unique interdisciplinary background spanning two Ph.D. degreesâ€”one in Computer Science and another in Applied Mathematicsâ€”I bridge theoretical foundations with practical implementations in AI and machine learning. My research has contributed to diverse fields including computer vision, medical data analysis, and urban management systems.

Before my current position, I worked as a Machine Learning Engineer at Magfa, developing automated web crawling systems and anomaly detection algorithms, and as an AI Researcher at Iran's National Elites Foundation, focusing on deep learning-based object detection using state-of-the-art architectures like YOLO, RCNN variants, and SSD.

I am passionate about making artificial intelligence more interpretable and accessible, with particular interest in how AI can address real-world challenges in healthcare, urban planning, and scientific research.
